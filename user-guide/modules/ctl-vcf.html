CTL / VCF (high-level explanation)

Some calculations in SurveyTools require a temperature correction factor for petroleum products. You may see this referred to as CTL (or, more generally, a volume correction factor sometimes called VCF).

What it is used for (in this app)

  • Adjusting volume between observed temperature and a reference temperature.
  • Supporting dependent calculations such as floating roof displacement (FRC).
  • Supporting some unit conversions (e.g. standard volume to barrels at a reference condition).

Important note about standards

The underlying industry standards and tables used in professional custody transfer are paid publications. This user guide does not reproduce any paid tables, copyrighted text, or claim full compliance/certification.

Best practice

  • Use SurveyTools as a calculation aid within your existing workflow.
  • Where required by your contract/procedure, cross-check with your official tables or certified systems.
  • If results look unexpected, verify your density basis (vacuum/air), temperature, and product group selection.

Implementation note (for maintainers): the app uses an internal CTL calculator (e.g. CTLcalc) and exposes native-ready facades in the shared module.
